# Add a TXT record (any provider)

Every DNS provider's dashboard looks a little different, but publishing a TXT record follows the same shape everywhere. If your provider is [Cloudflare](/docs/add-txt-record-cloudflare), [GoDaddy](/docs/add-txt-record-godaddy), [Vercel](/docs/add-txt-record-vercel), or [AWS Route 53](/docs/add-txt-record-route53), its own guide covers the exact screens — use this one for everything else.

The record DomainProof asks for is always two values, shown on the [hosted verification page](/docs/hosted-verification-page):

- **Host** (also called Name, or Record name) — something like `_acme-challenge.example.com`.
- **Value** (also called Content, or Data) — something like `acme-verify=abc123`.

## 1. Find your DNS provider's records page

Sign in wherever you manage DNS for the domain — usually the registrar you bought it from, or whichever host its nameservers point at (check your registrar's dashboard for the domain's current nameservers if you're not sure). Look for a section called "DNS", "DNS Records", "DNS Management", or "Zone Editor".

## 2. Add a new record, type TXT

Most providers have an "Add record" (or "Add new record") button, followed by a Type dropdown — choose `TXT`.

## 3. Enter the host and value exactly as shown

Paste the **Host**/**Name** and **Value** fields from DomainProof's record card into the matching fields on your provider's form.

One common gotcha: some providers want the host's full value (`_acme-challenge.example.com`), while others want only the part before your domain name (`_acme-challenge`) — they append the domain automatically. If your provider's Name/Host field says something like "without the domain name" or shows your domain as a fixed suffix next to the input, drop the trailing `.example.com` and enter just the prefix.

Leave TTL at its default — DomainProof doesn't require a specific value.

## 4. Save, then recheck

Save the record, then go back to the hosted verification page and click **Recheck** (or just wait — it polls automatically every 20 seconds). DNS changes can take anywhere from a few seconds to a few minutes to become visible, depending on your provider.
